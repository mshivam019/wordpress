Diogenes of Sinope

Diogenes, also known as Diogenes the Cynic, was a Greek philosopher and one of the founders of Cynic philosophy. Diogenes is someone who taught me the true meaning of living life in the purest form possible.Stories of Diogenes:

1. During his entire life he lived inside a wine barrel. The only possession that he had was a wooden bowl which he used for everything. Eventually he got rid of that bowl too. When he saw a child drinking water from hollow his hands, Diogenes threw away his cup and remarked, “A child has beaten me in plainness of living.” He then exclaimed: “Fool that I am, to have been carrying superfluous baggage all this time!”. 

                                      Diogenes and Plato

2. During one of the Plato’s lecture when he was discussing about the metaphysical ideal forms, In which he said all things have a pure essence , each thing has a form that define their true being. Diogenes who didn’t like metaphysical ideas challenged Plato and asked if the cup present also has some eternal perfect form somewhere to which Plato responded yes, infact the cup can take many different forms but their is perfect form of cupness that resides in our head. He then insulted Diogenes on his simple minded take of his complex ideas. Diogenes then responded what about the emptiness inside the cup, “Where is the ’emptiness’ which precedes this empty cup?”. Plato allowed himself a few moments to collect his thoughts, but Diogenes reached over and, tapping Plato’s head with his finger, said “I think you will find here is the ’emptiness'”.

                                Diogenes and Alexander 
3. One day Alexander the great went to meet Diogenes, who was relaxing on a field under the morning sun, Alexander asked Diogenes if there’s any favor he could do for him, he’d do it to which Diogenes replied yes by moving away since you are covering the sun. Shortly after Alexander declared if he wasn’t Alexander he’d want to be Diogenes to which Diogenes responded by saying “If I were not Diogenes, I would also wish to be Diogenes”. This story shows the encounter of two extremes , Alexander who had everything and Diogenes who had nothing but only one of them was truly free. Alexander was humbled by Diogenes as he realized things we value actually carries little satisfaction on it’s own and all the things Alexander had and others wanted meant nothing to Diogenes.

Diogenes work in history is known as “A Socrates gone mad”. They did not write their ideas they lived them instead. When he died he gave his body back to the earth as a way to return what he took.
